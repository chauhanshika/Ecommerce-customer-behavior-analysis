# Customer Behavior Analysis in E-commerce (SQL Project)

## Overview
This project presents a structured analysis of customer purchasing behavior in an e-commerce environment using SQL. The objective is to uncover meaningful patterns in user activity, revenue generation, and product performance, and translate these findings into actionable business insights.

This project was developed as part of the **SQL for Data Science** program by the **University of California, Davis (UCDavis)** on Coursera, and has been further refined and structured as a portfolio-ready data analytics project.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze customer purchasing behavior and engagement patterns  
- Understand revenue distribution across users  
- Identify key drivers influencing total revenue  
- Evaluate product category performance  
- Generate business insights and strategic recommendations  

---

## Project Workflow
The analysis follows a structured data analytics pipeline:

1. **Data Preparation**  
   - Data connection and setup  
   - Initial understanding of dataset structure  

2. **SQL Analysis**  
   - Querying and aggregating data  
   - Extracting key metrics such as revenue and user activity  

3. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**  
   - Descriptive statistics  
   - Trend identification and pattern discovery  

4. **Final Insights & Recommendations**  
   - Business interpretation of findings  
   - Strategic recommendations based on analysis

## Key Insights
- A small group of users contributes a significant portion of total revenue  
- Purchase frequency has a strong impact on overall revenue generation  
- Most users exhibit low engagement levels  
- Revenue is unevenly distributed, indicating high-value customer segments  
- Certain product categories outperform others in terms of revenue contribution

## Business Recommendations
- Focus on **retaining high-value customers** through targeted strategies  
- Encourage **repeat purchases** to increase customer lifetime value  
- Improve engagement for low-frequency users  
- Optimize and invest in **high-performing product categories**  
- Use data-driven strategies for better decision-making
